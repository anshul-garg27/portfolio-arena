About

I think about systems the way an architect thinks about buildings -- structure first, then the light. Every line of code is a decision about what matters, and I believe the best software is built at the intersection of rigorous engineering and genuine empathy for the people who use it.

At Walmart, I work on applications that power commerce for hundreds of millions of customers. The scale is immense, but the details still matter. A hundred-millisecond improvement in load time is not just a metric -- it is a moment of someone's day made imperceptibly smoother.

I am drawn to the craft of building things that last: clean architectures, thoughtful interfaces, code that communicates its intent. The kind of work where the elegance is invisible to everyone except those who look closely.

Tools change. Principles endure.
