Chapter One

The Beginning

Every great story begins with a spark. A flicker of curiosity that refuses to be extinguished...

Long before the campfire, long before the code, there was a kid who couldn't stop asking "how does this work?" -- staring at screens not with the glazed eyes of consumption, but with the sharp focus of someone trying to reverse-engineer the world.

Anshull Garg discovered early that the most powerful thing about software wasn't the machines -- it was the problems you could solve. Every line of code was an incantation. Every debugged error, a small victory against chaos.

The path was never straight. Good ones never are. But it was always forward.

Chapter Two

The First Quest

The early days. When every project felt like scaling a mountain with no map...

Armed with determination and a growing toolkit of languages and frameworks, Anshull set out into the wilderness of professional software development. The first projects were formative -- each one a trial by fire.

He learned that great software isn't built in isolation. It's forged in collaboration -- in whiteboard sessions that run long, in code reviews that sharpen thinking, in the quiet satisfaction of watching a feature ship and knowing it actually helps people.

The foundations were laid: clean architecture, relentless testing, and an obsession with user experience that would define everything to come.

But the real challenge was still ahead. A forge hot enough to temper steel.

Chapter Three

The Forge

They say you learn the most where the stakes are highest. At Walmart, the stakes touch millions of lives daily...

Joining Walmart as a Software Developer was like stepping into a forge -- the heat intense, the craft demanding, the scale unlike anything before. Here, code doesn't just run on a server; it powers an ecosystem that serves hundreds of millions of customers.

Working at this scale taught lessons no textbook could: how to design systems that breathe under immense load, how to ship with confidence when downtime is measured in dollars-per-second, and how to collaborate across teams spanning continents.

The forge didn't just shape the code. It shaped the engineer. Discipline. Ownership. The understanding that every commit is a promise to the people who depend on what you build.

From the forge, the weapons were drawn...
