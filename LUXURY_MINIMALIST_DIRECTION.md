## THE PHILOSOPHY

This is not "another minimalist portfolio." This is a **Bottega Veneta of developer portfolios** -- a site so intentionally crafted that every pixel radiates quiet confidence. The kind of portfolio where a hiring manager at Google, Apple, or Stripe stops scrolling, sits up straighter, and thinks: "This person understands craft."

We borrow from the design DNA of:
- **Apple** -- progressive disclosure, scroll-driven storytelling, typographic drama
- **Celine (Phoebe Philo era)** -- ultra-thin type, zero clutter, editorial restraint
- **Aesop** -- warm neutrals, grid precision, monochromatic harmony
- **Bottega Veneta** -- no logos needed; the craft IS the identity

The core principle: **Say less. Mean more. Let the work breathe.**

## 1. VISUAL IDENTITY

### Design Language
- **Editorial, not corporate.** The site should feel like a premium print magazine digitized, not a LinkedIn profile with better CSS.
- **Architectural whitespace.** Spacing is not emptiness -- it is the most expensive material on the page. Every margin is a deliberate decision.
- **Asymmetric balance.** Swiss-grid foundations with intentional asymmetry that creates visual tension and movement.
- **Photography-grade visual quality.** Any imagery (code screenshots, project visuals) is treated as art -- cropped, graded, and placed with gallery-level care.

### Design Rules
1. No element exists without purpose. If it does not inform, guide, or delight -- remove it.
2. Maximum 3 visual elements per viewport at any time.
3. Text never touches the edge of a container. Minimum 80px padding on desktop, 24px on mobile.
4. All transitions use custom easing curves (cubic-bezier), never `ease` or `linear`.
5. Interactive elements reveal themselves through behavior, not decoration (no outlines on buttons -- the cursor change and subtle opacity shift IS the affordance).

### Typography Rules

1. **Hero headings:** 72-128px. Let them dominate. One heading per viewport.
2. **Body text:** Always 18-20px minimum. Never 16px -- this is not a blog template.
3. **Line height:** 1.6 for body, 1.1-1.2 for display headings (tight leading on large type = tension and sophistication).
4. **Letter spacing:** Headings at -0.02em to -0.04em (tighter). Uppercase labels at +0.12em to +0.2em (tracked out).
5. **Paragraph width:** Maximum 65 characters. No exceptions. This is a readability non-negotiable.
6. **Contrast mixing:** Always pair a serif display with a sans-serif body (or vice versa). The contrast between them creates visual richness without adding visual noise.

## 4. LAYOUT CONCEPTS

### The Grid
- **Desktop:** 12-column grid. Content lives in the center 8 columns (66% of viewport width). The remaining 4 columns are pure whitespace. This is the "luxury breathing room" -- the digital equivalent of a high-ceiling room.
- **Content max-width:** 1200px, centered.
- **Section spacing:** 200-300px between major sections. This is aggressive. This is intentional. Each section feels like turning a page.
- **Asymmetric content placement:** Text blocks align to the left 5 columns. Images/visuals float to the right 6 columns. They never symmetrically center -- this creates the editorial tension that makes the layout feel curated, not generated.

### Section Structure

```
[HERO] ............ Full viewport. Single statement. Nothing else.
                    200px gap
[ABOUT] ........... Asymmetric two-column. Portrait left (if used), text right.
                    Text is brief -- 3-4 sentences maximum. Every word earned.
                    300px gap
[EXPERIENCE] ...... Timeline-style, but horizontal. Scroll horizontally
                    within this section. Each role is a "card" with generous
                    padding, a thin 1px rule separator, and Walmart's story
                    told in 2 sentences max.
                    300px gap
[WORK] ............ The centerpiece. Each project gets its OWN full-viewport
                    section. Scroll through them like gallery rooms.
                    Project title (massive serif), one-line description,
                    tech stack (small caps), and a single hero image.
                    Click to expand into a case study.
                    200px gap
[CAPABILITIES] .... A single, elegant list. No progress bars. No percentages.
                    No "90% React." Instead, clean categorized lists:
                    "Languages | Frameworks | Infrastructure | Design"
                    in a horizontal rule-separated layout.
                    200px gap
[CONTACT] ......... One line: "Let's talk." An email address. That's it.
                    The confidence of having nothing else here IS the flex.
```

### Mobile Philosophy
- Mobile is not a compressed desktop. It is its own experience.
- Single column. Even more whitespace proportionally.
- Touch targets 48px minimum.
- Horizontal scroll sections become vertical stacks with swipe hints.
- Typography scales down gracefully: hero text at 40-48px, body at 17px.

## 5. MICRO-INTERACTIONS

Every interaction should feel like touching a well-made physical object. No bounces. No wobbles. No confetti. **Precision.**

### Navigation
- **Menu items:** On hover, text opacity shifts from 0.5 to 1.0 over 300ms with a custom cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1.0). A thin 1px underline draws itself from left to right.
- **Current section indicator:** A small dot (6px) that slides along a vertical track on the right edge of the viewport, like a scroll position indicator in a luxury e-reader.
- **Menu appearance:** Navigation is hidden by default. Appears after 100px of scroll. Fades in with a subtle upward translation (transform: translateY(8px) to 0).

### Cursor
- Custom cursor: A small circle (12px) with a 1px border in the accent color. On hovering over interactive elements, it smoothly scales to 40px with the label text appearing inside ("view", "email", "read"). This single detail will make the portfolio feel bespoke.

### Scroll-Triggered Reveals
- **Text:** Each line of a text block fades in sequentially with a 50ms stagger. Opacity 0 to 1, translateY(20px) to 0. Duration: 600ms. Easing: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1).
- **Images:** Clip-path reveal. Images start with `clip-path: inset(0 100% 0 0)` and reveal to `clip-path: inset(0 0% 0 0)` over 1200ms. Like a curtain pulling aside.
- **Horizontal rules:** Draw from left to right, width 0% to 100%, over 800ms when scrolled into view.

### Project Cards (Work Section)
- On hover: the project image subtly scales (1.0 to 1.03) over 600ms. The project title shifts 8px to the right. A thin line extends from the title toward the image.
- On click: the card expands to full viewport with a smooth FLIP animation (800ms).

### Page Transitions
- If using multi-page routing: a warm-tinted overlay (accent color at 3% opacity) sweeps across the viewport left-to-right over 400ms, content fades, new content fades in.
- Prefer single-page with smooth scroll anchoring over multi-page routing.

### Loading State
- No spinner. Instead, the site name renders letter by letter in the display serif font, each letter fading in with 80ms stagger. Once loaded, the text gracefully scales down and moves to its final position in the header (a la Apple's boot logo animation).

### Hover on External Links
- Small arrow icon (45-degree) smoothly rotates from 0 to 45 degrees and translates 2px right, 2px up. Communicates "this goes somewhere" without tooltip clutter.

## 6. UNIQUE FEATURES

These are the elements that elevate this portfolio from "clean" to "unforgettable."

### 6.1 -- The Living Grid (Background Detail)
A barely-visible grid pattern (2% opacity) on the background that responds to cursor position with a subtle radial gradient highlight. It does not move. It does not animate aggressively. It simply... acknowledges the visitor's presence. Like the way a luxury hotel's concierge looks up the moment you walk in. Implemented with a CSS radial-gradient that follows the cursor via a lightweight mousemove listener.

### 6.2 -- The Scroll Progress Narrative
Instead of a traditional progress bar, a single word changes in the navigation as the user scrolls through sections:

```
"Hello." -> "Background." -> "Experience." -> "Work." -> "Skills." -> "Let's talk."
```

The word transitions with a split-text animation -- old word's letters fall away downward, new word's letters rise from below. Fluid, text-only, zero visual clutter.

### 6.3 -- Time-Aware Design
The palette subtly shifts based on the visitor's local time:
- **Morning (6AM-12PM):** Slightly cooler tones. Background shifts 2 degrees toward blue.
- **Afternoon (12PM-6PM):** Warm, as designed -- the "golden hour" version.
- **Evening (6PM-12AM):** Background deepens slightly. Accent color warms. Simulating late-day light.
- **Night (12AM-6AM):** Option to flip to a dark mode variant -- deep warm charcoal (#1C1917) background with cream text. Automatic, no toggle. Luxury is when things just work.

The shift is imperceptible in a single visit -- it is noticed only when someone visits at different times and thinks "something feels different." That is the level of craft we are pursuing.

### 6.4 -- Code as Art
In the Experience or Skills section, actual code snippets from Anshul's work (or representative samples) are displayed not as typical code blocks, but as **typographic art pieces** -- syntax-highlighted in the site's accent palette (terracotta for keywords, gold for strings, stone for comments), with the code itself set in a custom monospace at large scale (28-36px), displayed one function at a time with a line-by-line scroll reveal. The code is not there to be read in full -- it is there to say "I write beautiful code." It is the developer equivalent of Bottega Veneta showing the intrecciato weave close-up.

### 6.5 -- The Detail Page Experience
When a project is selected, the transition is not a page load -- it is an **expansion**. The card grows, background blurs, and the case study loads within the same scroll context. The case study itself follows a strict structure:

```
[Project Title -- massive serif]
[One-sentence summary]
[Role | Timeline | Impact -- small caps row]
[Full-bleed hero image]
[The Challenge -- 2-3 sentences]
[The Approach -- 2-3 sentences]
[Key Technical Decisions -- bullet points, each on its own line with generous spacing]
[Results -- quantified where possible]
[Close button -- just an "X" or "Back" in the nav that smoothly contracts back to the grid]
```

### 6.6 -- Sound Design (Optional, Avant-Garde)
A subtle, optional audio layer: on first visit, a small prompt asks "Experience with sound?" If yes, interactions produce extremely subtle audio feedback -- a soft click on navigation, a whisper of paper when scrolling between sections. Mixed at barely-audible levels. This is the kind of detail that gets written about. Nobody does this. It must be executed with extreme restraint or not at all.

## 7. HERO SECTION CONCEPT

### "The Entrance"

The hero section is the first 3 seconds. It must be perfect.

**What the visitor sees on load:**

A warm parchment background (`#F5F0E8`) fills the entire viewport. Nothing else for 400ms -- just warmth and color. Then:

1. **The Name** -- "Anshul Garg" -- appears in PP Editorial New Light at 96px (desktop) / 44px (mobile). Positioned in the left-center of the viewport (left-aligned to the content grid, vertically centered). Each letter fades in with a 60ms stagger, left to right. Total reveal: ~720ms. The type has -0.03em letter-spacing. It looks like it was typeset by hand.

2. **The Title** -- 400ms after the name completes, a single line appears below in Neue Haas Grotesk Display Light, all uppercase, tracked at +0.2em, 14px, in the muted stone color: `SOFTWARE DEVELOPER AT WALMART`. Fades in with a subtle translateY(12px) to translateY(0) over 500ms. No icons. No logos. The restraint is the point.

3. **The Statement** -- 300ms later, below the title, in Neue Haas Grotesk Text at 20px, regular weight, in the primary text color, a single sentence: *"Building systems that serve millions, designed with the care of serving one."* Opacity 0 to 1 over 600ms.

4. **The Scroll Indicator** -- At the bottom center of the viewport, a thin vertical line (1px wide, 40px tall, accent color) gently pulses in opacity (0.3 to 0.7) in a slow 3-second loop. No text. No arrow. No "scroll down." The line is enough. Beneath it, barely visible at 30% opacity: a single word in small caps -- "explore."

**What is NOT on the hero:**
- No hamburger menu (navigation appears after scroll begins)
- No social icons
- No photo (the name is the hero)
- No background image or video
- No gradient
- No particles, mesh, or 3D effect

**The emptiness is the statement.** In a world of portfolios screaming for attention with animated backgrounds and floating shapes, this hero says: "I don't need gimmicks. My name is enough."

### Dark Mode Variant (Night Hours)
Same layout. Background becomes `#1C1917` (warm near-black). Text becomes `#F5F0E8` (warm parchment). The accent becomes a warmer terracotta `#D4845A`. The effect is like walking into a dimly-lit gallery. Immediate mood shift.

## 8. SCROLL EXPERIENCE

### The Narrative Arc

Scrolling through this portfolio should feel like reading a beautifully designed monograph -- each section is a chapter, and the whitespace between them is the page turn.

### Section-by-Section Scroll Choreography

**0-100vh: HERO (Sticky)**
- Hero content stays pinned.
- As user begins scrolling (0-50vh of travel), the hero text slowly fades (opacity 1 to 0) while translating upward by 30px.
- The scroll indicator line extends downward, becoming a thin vertical rule that connects the hero to the next section.
- Background color remains constant.

**100vh-130vh: THE BREATHING SPACE**
- 30vh of pure background color. No content. This is the "page turn."
- During this scroll, the navigation bar fades in at the top (fixed position, background transparent).
- The navigation contains only: the word "Anshul" (left, in the display font at 18px) and section labels (right, in small caps grotesque at 12px, spaced 40px apart).

**130vh-230vh: ABOUT**
- Text enters from the bottom with a staggered line-by-line reveal.
- A thin horizontal rule (1px, accent color) draws itself across the top of the section over 800ms.
- Content: 3-4 sentences about Anshul's philosophy as a developer. No "I am a passionate..." cliches. Instead, something with voice: *"I think about systems the way an architect thinks about buildings -- structure first, then the light."*
- An optional small portrait photo (black and white, high contrast) is positioned asymmetrically in the right gutter, slightly overlapping the grid boundary. It fades in after the text, with a clip-path reveal from bottom to top.

**230vh-430vh: EXPERIENCE (The Horizontal Moment)**
- This section introduces a horizontal scroll within the vertical flow.
- The section title "Experience" appears (large serif, full-width) and pins.
- As the user continues scrolling vertically, the content scrolls horizontally -- each role presented as a full-viewport-width panel:

  ```
  Panel 1: WALMART
  "Software Developer | 2023 -- Present"
  [2-3 sentences about impact]
  [Key technologies in small caps]
  ```

  ```
  Panel 2: PREVIOUS ROLE (if applicable)
  Same structure.
  ```

- Panels transition with a crossfade + slight horizontal translate (200ms offset).
- A thin progress line at the bottom of this section fills left-to-right as the user scrolls through the panels.
- After the last panel, vertical scrolling resumes.

**430vh-830vh: WORK (The Gallery)**
- Each project occupies 100vh.
- Projects are pinned: as the user scrolls, the current project fades out while the next slides up from behind it (parallax stack effect, like cards in a deck being revealed).
- Each project viewport contains:
  - Project number ("01", "02") in thin serif, top-left corner, 120px, at 15% opacity
  - Project name in large serif, center-left
  - One-line description below in grotesque
  - Tech stack as a comma-separated list in small caps below that
  - Hero image of the project, positioned to the right, with a subtle parallax offset (moves at 0.8x scroll speed relative to text)
- A subtle visual cue: a thin line connecting each project number, forming a continuous thread through the section

**830vh-930vh: CAPABILITIES**
- No bars. No charts. No percentages. Skills are presented as text.
- Four columns (2 on mobile):
  ```
  LANGUAGES         FRAMEWORKS         INFRASTRUCTURE      PRACTICES
  JavaScript        React              AWS                 System Design
  TypeScript        Node.js            Docker              CI/CD
  Python            Next.js            Kubernetes          Testing
  Java              Express            Terraform           Agile
  ```
- Each column header has the accent color.
- Items fade in with a staggered reveal, column by column (left to right), with 30ms delay between items.
- At the bottom: a single sentence in italic serif: *"Tools change. Principles don't."*

**930vh-1000vh: CONTACT**
- The entire viewport darkens slightly (background shifts to the secondary background color).
- A single line appears center-viewport: *"Let's talk."* in the display serif at 72px.
- Below it, after 400ms: an email address in the grotesque font, underlined with the accent color. On hover, the underline thickness animates from 1px to 2px and shifts to accent color.
- Below that, after another 400ms: three text links (no icons) -- "GitHub", "LinkedIn", "Resume" -- spaced 40px apart, in small caps.
- That is the entire section. The restraint says more than a contact form ever could.

**1000vh-1030vh: FOOTER**
- Barely there. A thin 1px rule. Below it, in 12px muted text: "(c) 2026 Anshul Garg" on the left, "Built with care" on the right.
- 80px of padding below. End.

### Scroll Physics
- Smooth scroll enabled site-wide via CSS `scroll-behavior: smooth` and a lightweight JS library (Lenis) for buttery scroll interpolation.
- All scroll-triggered animations use `IntersectionObserver` for performance (no scroll event listeners on every frame).
- Parallax effects use `transform: translate3d()` for GPU acceleration.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` media query disables all animations and reveals all content statically. Accessibility is non-negotiable.

## 10. THE ARGUMENT: WHY LUXURY MINIMALIST IS THE BEST DIRECTION

### Against "Flashy Interactive"
Sites built around Three.js scenes, particle effects, and complex WebGL degrade on mobile, load slowly, and often feel like tech demos rather than professional portfolios. They impress developers but alienate the hiring managers, design leads, and CTOs who actually make decisions. A luxury minimalist site impresses EVERYONE.

### Against "Dark Hacker Aesthetic"
Terminal-green-on-black portfolios say "I am a developer" but nothing else. They are a dime a dozen. They show technical identity but no design sensibility, no understanding of user experience, and no personality beyond the code. A Walmart software developer who presents with luxury minimalism demonstrates range -- they understand systems AND aesthetics, code AND craft.

### Against "Playful/Colorful"
Fun, illustrated portfolios with bouncy animations work for junior designers at creative agencies. For a software developer at a Fortune 1 company who wants to signal senior-level maturity and readiness for principal/staff-level roles at top firms, the message must be: **"I build things that matter, and I obsess over how they're built."** Luxury minimalism is the only aesthetic that conveys this without words.

### The Unique Advantage
Most developer portfolios exist in one of two lanes: "I can code cool things" (dark, techy, effects-heavy) or "I'm creative" (colorful, illustrated, playful). The luxury minimalist direction occupies a THIRD lane that almost nobody is in: **"I have impeccable taste."**

Taste is the scarcest quality in tech. When a portfolio demonstrates taste -- through typography, spacing, color restraint, and interaction design -- it signals something money cannot buy and tutorials cannot teach. It signals that this developer will not just build features; they will build features that FEEL right.

### The Walmart Narrative
Walmart serves 240+ million customers. The systems Anshul works on operate at a scale most developers never touch. A luxury minimalist portfolio reframes this: it says "I work at massive scale, and I still care about every detail." The contrast between Walmart's scale and the portfolio's intimacy is itself a powerful narrative device.
